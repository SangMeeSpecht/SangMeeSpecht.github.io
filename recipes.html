Bokkeumbap: Fried Rice - TV Show

This recipe comes from one of my favorite Korean TV shows (KDrama), called "Let's Eat." In episode 14 of season 2, one of the main characters, a foodie named Dae Young, explains that, "Cooking is the taste of fire." With the little ingredients left in his friends fridge, he uses the slight scorching of the heat to enhance the flavor of the fried rice. The reason we use day old rice is because it has less water in it, so when it's fried it'll get chewier, which is the texture you want. Trust me, it tastes better than it sounds!

Ingredients

  • 1 T butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 chopped green onion
  • 2 C day old, cooked rice
  • ketchup

Directions (serves 2)

  1. Melt the butter in a medium skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Crack the eggs into the skillet and immediately swirl the eggs around with a wooden spoon until they are fully cooked.
  3. Remove eggs from skillet and pour in the sesame oil over medium heat.
  4. Pour in the chopped green onion and cook for 1 minute.
  5. Remove the onions from the skillet and stir in the rice. Break apart the rice until it's spread evenly across the skillet.
  6. After 2 minutes, mix the eggs and green onions back to the skillet and turn the burner up to high. Cook for 3 more minutes until the rice turns golden brown on the bottom.
  7. Turn off the burner and put the rice in a bowl. Finish it off with a nice drizzle of ketchup.
